# Failure Modes and Antipatterns

## 1. Transcript sludge
The output includes too many quotes, too many components, or too much raw meeting content.

## 2. Generic summary mode
The report becomes a broad meeting recap instead of task-relevant technical analysis.

## 3. Glossary mode
The report defines components without explaining why they matter.

## 4. Overinclusion
Too many components are included, making the report hard to scan and weakly prioritized.

## 5. Fake precision
Confidence scores or overstated claims make the output sound more certain than the transcript supports.

## 6. Inference leakage
Inferred relationships are written as if they were explicit facts.

## 7. Weak evidence
Claims are made with little or no supporting transcript excerpts.

## 8. Ownership clutter
Too many names are included, or names are listed without a clear action value.

## 9. Ambiguity suppression
The report hides missing context instead of surfacing it.

## 10. Empty next steps
The report identifies ambiguity but fails to translate it into concrete follow-up actions.

## 11. Rigid template misuse
All subsections are filled even when they add no value, creating noise.

## 12. Non-technical drift
The analysis spends too much space on meeting logistics, social chatter, or status updates with no architectural value.
