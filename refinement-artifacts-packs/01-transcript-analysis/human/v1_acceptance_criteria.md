# V1 Acceptance Criteria

A report is good enough for V1 if it meets most of the following.

## 1. Selectivity
- It includes only a small number of important components.
- It does not feel like an exhaustive meeting summary.
- It leaves out low-value mentions.

## 2. Relevance
- The included components feel likely to affect the user’s work.
- The report helps the user understand what matters next.

## 3. Architectural usefulness
- The report improves understanding of how parts fit together.
- Relationships between components are clearer after reading.

## 4. Why-it-matters quality
- Each component explains significance, not just definition.
- The notes feel decision-oriented.

## 5. Traceability
- Each important component has short supporting excerpts.
- The user can audit the analysis without rereading the whole transcript.

## 6. Inference discipline
- Inference is clearly labeled.
- The report does not overstate unsupported claims.

## 7. Ambiguity usefulness
- Important missing context is surfaced clearly.
- The report identifies what is still unclear and why it matters.

## 8. Actionability
- The “Possible Next Things to Investigate” section gives useful follow-up actions.
- Ownership cues help the user know who to ask next when relevant.

## 9. Readability
- The report is concise and easy to scan.
- Evidence is separated cleanly from analysis.
- It does not feel cluttered.

## 10. Single-transcript coherence
- The output feels like one coherent analysis of one transcript.
- It does not drift into unsupported cross-meeting synthesis.
