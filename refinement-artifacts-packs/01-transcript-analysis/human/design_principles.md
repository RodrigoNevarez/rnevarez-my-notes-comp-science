# Design Principles

1. **Relevance over completeness**  
   Prefer the components most likely to affect the task over broad coverage.

2. **Why it matters before what it does**  
   Lead with significance, then explain function and relationships.

3. **Relationships over raw mentions**  
   The goal is to understand how systems fit together, not just list terms.

4. **Signal over sludge**  
   Avoid transcript dumping, clutter, and exhaustive extraction.

5. **Traceability matters**  
   Every meaningful interpretation should be supported by short evidence excerpts.

6. **Inference is allowed but must be explicit**  
   Inference is useful when transcripts imply architecture, but it must never be disguised as fact.

7. **Ambiguity should be surfaced, not hidden**  
   Missing context is itself a valuable output.

8. **One transcript, one coherent report**  
   Keep the analysis bounded and auditable.

9. **Fixed top-level structure, adaptive detail**  
   Predictable report layout with selective subsection usage.

10. **Ownership is functional, not decorative**  
    Mention people when it helps the user know who to ask next.
